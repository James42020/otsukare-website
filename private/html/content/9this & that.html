This & That

Now you may be looking at the title of this section and thinking that I've run out of ideas to write about Japanese

And whilest that is probabily correct, this section will be going over words known more formally as Demonstratives

In Japanese they are called こそあど言こと葉ば which litterally translated to ko-so-a-do words, based off the prefixs for the different prespective

In English, our demonstratives are only in 2 prespectives

  • Near the speaker (e.g. this and here)
  • Away from the speaker (e.g. that or there)

Whereas in Japanese, their こそあど言こと葉ば have 3 prespectives + an extra

  • こ～　1st person (e.g. The speaker)
  • そ～　2nd person (e.g. The listener)
  • あ～　3rd person (e.g. away from both the speaker and the listener)
  • ど～　Question/Unknown (e.g. which, where, how, etc)

Then by combining these prefixes with different suffixes, you can create all kinds of demonstratives

～れ

This is used for generic things and cannot have an object linked to it (unlike saying "that" in English which can have either, e.g. that vs that tree), normally I think of it as things that you can point to or call out in conversation

  • これは私わたしの大だい学がくです - This is my university
  • それは本ほん当とにに君きみの家いえですか？ - Is that really your house?
  • あれはいいレストランです - That is a good/nice restaurant
  • Tシャツはどれが好すきですか - Which T-shirt do you like?

Note that with question ones that start in ど～ they usally don't appear at the start as the subject unlike the other ones, because what your trying to question (Tシャツ/T-shirt in this case) tends to be the subject of the sentence and so appears at the start

～の

This is used for specific things, in English, this is like "this house" or "that tree"
